U.S. oil and gas worker fatalities 2003-14

From 2003 to 2014, more than 1,300 oil and gas workers have been killed on the job. With 45 fatalities in that time period, Colorado ranks seventh in the country. Texas has the most worker deaths, with 538 killed on the job. Note here on difficulty of pinning these figures down with link to sidebar story about that.

Source: Bureau of Labor Statistics, Denver Post research

Hazardous occupations 2014

In 2014, 181 workers in the mining, quarrying and natural gas extraction sector died on the job, making it the ninth most dangerous sector for workers in the country. With 874 on-the-job deaths, construction workers had the highest fatality count. However, when measured by rate per 100,000 workers, the mining, quarrying and natural gas extraction sector ranked second (14.1) behind only the agriculture, forestry, fishing and hunting sector (24.9).
